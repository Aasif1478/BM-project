As part of our biomaterials research project, we designed and created a functional model of the Jarvik-7 artificial heart. This device is a milestone in biomedical engineering, representing the first permanent total artificial heart implanted in a human. Our model replicates its basic function using pump mechanisms and wires, focusing on illustrating the core engineering behind it.

üîß How the Model Was Made

  • Miniature pumps to simulate the two ventricles (right and left).
  • Flexible tubing to mimic arteries and veins.
  • Wiring and control switches for rhythmic pumping.
  • 3D-printed or hand-molded chambers for heart compartments.
  • Transparent casing to demonstrate internal mechanisms visually.
